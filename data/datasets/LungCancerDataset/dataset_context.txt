DATASET: Lung Cancer Symptom Dataset
DOMAIN: Medical / Oncology
SOURCE: Kaggle — JIS College of Engineering
DESCRIPTION: Symptom-based lung cancer risk dataset with 3 000 anonymized patient records. Each patient has 15 binary symptom indicators plus age, gender and a lung cancer diagnosis label.
SAMPLE: Each row represents one patient observation with recorded symptoms and diagnosis outcome.

COLUMNS:
- id: Unique patient identifier. Not a clinical measurement.
- GENDER: Patient sex. M = Male, F = Female.
- AGE: Patient age at time of recording. Unit: years. Range: approximately 30–80.
- SMOKING: History of smoking.
- YELLOW_FINGERS: Yellowing of finger tips (nicotine staining, associated with heavy smoking).
- ANXIETY: Patient reports anxiety.
- PEER_PRESSURE: Patient reports peer pressure (social smoking context).
- CHRONIC_DISEASE: Patient has a chronic disease (e.g. COPD).
- FATIGUE: Patient reports chronic fatigue.
- ALLERGY: Patient has known allergies.
- WHEEZING: Patient reports wheezing (audible breathing difficulty).
- ALCOHOL_CONSUMING: Patient consumes alcohol.
- COUGHING: Patient reports persistent coughing.
- SHORTNESS_OF_BREATH: Patient reports shortness of breath.
- SWALLOWING_DIFFICULTY: Patient reports difficulty swallowing (dysphagia).
- CHEST_PAIN: Patient reports chest pain.
- LUNG_CANCER: Diagnosis outcome. YES = lung cancer confirmed, NO = not confirmed.

CATEGORIES:
- GENDER: M = Male, F = Female
- LUNG_CANCER: YES = cancer confirmed, NO = not confirmed
- All binary symptom columns (SMOKING, YELLOW_FINGERS, ANXIETY, PEER_PRESSURE,
  CHRONIC_DISEASE, FATIGUE, ALLERGY, WHEEZING, ALCOHOL_CONSUMING, COUGHING,
  SHORTNESS_OF_BREATH, SWALLOWING_DIFFICULTY, CHEST_PAIN):
  1 = symptom absent / not reported, 2 = symptom present / reported

DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE:
- SMOKING and YELLOW_FINGERS are directly associated with tobacco use and elevated lung cancer risk
- WHEEZING, COUGHING, SHORTNESS_OF_BREATH, and CHEST_PAIN are the primary respiratory symptoms of lung cancer
- SWALLOWING_DIFFICULTY (dysphagia) can indicate tumor pressure on the esophagus — a later-stage symptom
- CHRONIC_DISEASE (especially COPD) is a known comorbidity and risk amplifier for lung cancer
- ANXIETY and PEER_PRESSURE reflect behavioral/social risk context
- Clusters with LUNG_CANCER purity above 85 % are high-risk patient profiles
- Clusters without cancer (LUNG_CANCER=NO, high purity) with multiple active symptoms may represent misdiagnosed or pre-diagnostic cases worth attention
- AGE is the only continuous variable; higher age generally correlates with higher cancer incidence
